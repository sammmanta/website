Skills and abilities come first —Before technique and tactics

“The best skill we can learn as children is the skill of learning.”

By Juan Paulo García
Written by Samanta García

Part I

How does the attitude of learning influence the way we play soccer?

Skills and abilities are pillars in the development of an integrated physicality; they are part of both sports training and everyday life. We need to ask ourselves where soccer training is failing these days and how we can learn the art of learning.

Foremost, I would like to emphasize that I am referring to skills and abilities, not only as the capacity to perform a task effectively, but also as the convergence of fundamental values in the process of teaching and reinforcing what has been learned throughout an athlete's career. The ability to learn is a hidden capacity waiting to be awakened and to face the unknown, the new.

Nowadays, self-knowledge and the learning process are often relegated in the pursuit of results, so we completely forget that human beings, who are always learning, must recognize themselves, know which of their strengths help them to be more effective in any learning process, especially when we talk about playing soccer.

Skills and abilities are not only equivalent to innate talent, but also to the environment and context. In other words, they are not outside the player's reality. It is thanks to the difficulties and adversities that life itself offers that skills and abilities become a treasure that players can awaken and strengthen in training.

In this sense, skills and abilities attack several fronts: physical, mental, and attitudinal. The path of learning is not a straight line; it is full of mistakes that are part of the finesse of learning fully, with a proactive attitude beyond following guidelines.

Skills and abilities, ultimately, are capacities that make a player stand out for their own autonomy, as they develop resourcefulness and enthusiasm for learning conscientiously with an open and willing mind.

I firmly believe in a creative and holistic methodology, enriched by the practice of sport and life. I make these reflections because I have witnessed and guided the learning processes of numerous children and adolescents, each with their own expectations, challenges, and strengths. Likewise, I make these reflections because I recognize the limitations of time and space that athletes and coaches face today.
